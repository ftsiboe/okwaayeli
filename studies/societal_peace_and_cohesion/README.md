### Abstract

Conflict and weak social cohesion are widely recognised as constraints on agricultural performance, but rigorous farm-level evidence from peaceful contexts that nonetheless display meaningful subnational variation in cohesion is scarce. Using the community modules of GLSS6 and GLSS7, this study constructs a Peace and Social Cohesion Index (PCSI) for Ghanaian farming locales and links it to farm-level technology adoption and technical efficiency through statistical matching and a meta-stochastic frontier framework. *Full abstract to follow once v001 lands.*
